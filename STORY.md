## 1. The One-Line Pitch (Logline)

> *A stray cat is taken in by a struggling family and, realising how hard they're fighting just to keep their home, quietly sets out to earn money for them — one caught mouse and one good deed at a time.*

**Theme:** Love is shown through small, steady actions. You don't have to be big to save the people you love.

**Feeling we want the player to have:** warm, hopeful, a little teary, proud. Cozy — never stressful or cruel.

---

## 2. Tone & Inspiration

- **Tone:** gentle, heartfelt, cozy realism. Hard times are real, but the game is kind. No gore, no cruelty to the cat.
- **Vibe references** (for mood only — change freely): *Stray*, *A Street Cat Named Bob*, *Stardew Valley*, *Unpacking*, Pixar's quieter moments.
- **Realism goal:** the *emotions* and the *daily struggle* feel real (bills, cold houses, tired parents) — even if the cat doing jobs is a sweet fantasy.

---

## 3. The Protagonist — Your Cat

**You choose your cat at the start**, from **10 different cats at the shelter** (see the next section). Your choice changes only the cat's **name and appearance** — the personality, story, and dialogue stay exactly the same. Every player gets the same heartfelt journey, just with the cat *they* fell in love with.

- **Default / signature cat:** `Penny`, a ginger cat (matches your current 3D model) — the name ties into the title "Paws & Pennies."
- **Shared backstory (all 10 cats):** a shelter cat who has known the hard side of life — hunger, cold, being alone. That's *why* your cat understands what the family is going through. (This stays the same whichever cat you pick.)
- **Personality:** quiet, observant, determined, secretly soft-hearted. Doesn't meow much — *acts* instead.
- **The cat's "voice":** the player hears the cat's inner thoughts in short, simple lines (e.g. *"This family has so little… but they shared it with me. I'll find a way to give back."*).
- **Arc:** from *a shelter cat who only knew how to survive* → to *a member of a family who learns how to give*.

## ⭐ The Adoption — Choosing Your Cat (Opening Scene) 🏠🐾

> This is now the **very first scene** of the game, before anything else.

### The scene
The Miller family walks into the **local cat shelter** on a grey afternoon. Money is tight, but Lily has been begging for weeks, and Elena finally said yes — *"One. We can manage one."* They walk past rows of little cages, each with a cat looking up at them. The player is given **10 cats to choose from** — each with a **name, a different look, and a one-line "hello."** The player picks one. That cat is lifted gently out, Lily's face lights up, and **that cat becomes the hero of the whole game.**

- **The emotional beat:** the family choosing a cat they can barely afford to feed — and the cat, who's been waiting and waiting for someone, finally being chosen. *"You came back for me?"*
- **Lily's line on the pick:** "That one, Mum. That one's been looking right at me the whole time."

### ✅ Important: the choice only changes name + looks
Whichever cat you pick, the rest of the game is **identical** — same story, same dialogue, same family. All the game does is:
1. Use the **chosen cat's name** everywhere the cat is referred to.
2. Apply the **chosen cat's colours** to the 3D cat model.

This keeps it simple to build and means you only have to write the story once.

## 4. The Family — The Millers 🏠

> The emotional centre of the game. The whole point is making *their* lives better. Keep them lovable and flawed-but-good.

### Elena Miller — the Mum *(rename freely)*
- Works double shifts [as a cleaner / at a diner]. Exhausted but never bitter.
- The one who said "yes" to keeping the cat even when they could barely feed themselves: *"There's always room for one more heart in this house."*
- **Her struggle:** carrying the family on her back, hiding how scared she is.
- **Her arc:** slowly allowed to rest and smile again as money comes in.

### Daniel Miller — the Dad *(rename freely)*
- Recently laid off [from the factory / workshop]. Spends his days job-hunting and fixing things around the house.
- **His struggle:** feels like a failure for not providing. Quietly ashamed.
- **His arc:** regains his pride — and eventually uses the breathing room the cat buys them to [start a small repair business / get back on his feet].

### Lily Miller — the Daughter (age ~8) *(rename freely)*
- The one who **found the cat** and begged to keep it. The cat's closest bond.
- Pure-hearted, talks to the cat like a best friend, tells it all the family's secrets.
- **Her struggle:** doesn't fully understand why there's no heating / why she can't have what other kids have — but she *feels* the sadness in the house.
- **Her arc:** her laughter coming back is one of the clearest signs the family is healing.
- **She's your narrator/guide:** Lily often tells you (the cat) what the family needs next.

### Noah Miller — the Son (age ~12) *(rename freely)*
- A bit withdrawn, embarrassed about being poor, hides it with a tough front.
- **His struggle:** getting teased at school; growing up too fast.
- **His arc:** opens up, starts to hope again, becomes protective of the cat.

> *(Optional family member you might add: a **Grandma/Grandpa** who tells stories and gives the cat advice; or a baby that the family is straining to provide for.)*

## 5. The Neighbourhood — Supporting Cast & Job-Givers

> These are the people who **pay the cat** for work. Each one is a small story. They already exist in your code — here they get personalities.

### Mrs. Chen — the kind grocer 🛒
- Elderly widow who runs the little corner shop. Soft-spoken, always has a treat.
- **Jobs:** carry/fetch dropped groceries, scare mice out of her storeroom.
- **Her warmth:** she figures out what the cat is doing for the Millers and starts quietly slipping in extra coins.

### Old Tom — the grumpy retired sailor ⚓ *(note: different from "Daniel" the dad)*
- Lives alone with a [boat / shed]. Gruff on the outside, lonely on the inside.
- **Jobs:** clear the rats out of his shed; keep him company.
- **His arc:** the cat melts his grumpiness; he becomes a loyal friend and one of your best-paying jobs.

### Farmer Bob — the big-hearted farmer 🌾
- Runs a small farm/barn at the edge of the neighbourhood. Overrun with mice.
- **Jobs:** the main **mouse-catching grounds** — lots of mice, steady coins.
- **His warmth:** pays in coins *and* in food he sends home to the Millers.

## 6. The Core Conflict (the stakes)

The Millers are **behind on rent.** Mr. Crick gives them a deadline: *pay what's owed by [the end of winter / 30 days], or the family loses the house.*

This is the engine of the whole game:
- It gives the coins a **purpose** (not just a score — you're saving their home).
- It creates a **deadline** that builds tension without being cruel.
- It lets the house **visibly improve** as you hit milestones.

> 💡 You can soften or harden this. Realistic but kind: the family never gets thrown out on screen — the tension is the *fear* of it, and the joy is beating it.

## 7. Story Structure — 5 Acts

> Each act is tied to a money milestone and a visible change in the house (see Section 8).

### ACT 1 — "A New Home"
**Opens at the cat shelter** (see the ⭐ Adoption section above): the family chooses one of 10 cats, and the player picks their hero. They take the cat home despite having almost nothing. That first night, the cat watches them share their small dinner, hears the parents whisper about money after the kids sleep, and **decides: I'm going to help.**
*(Goal: choose your cat, learn to move, catch your first mouse, earn your first coin.)*

### ACT 2 — "First Coins"
The cat learns the neighbourhood. Meets Mrs. Chen, Old Tom, Farmer Bob. Does first jobs. The family is baffled and delighted to find coins appearing. First small fix to the house (the fence, the leaky roof).
*(Goal: build trust with the neighbours, earn steadily, see the first home improvement.)*

### ACT 3 — "The Deadline"
Mr. Crick arrives. The family learns they could lose the house by [end of winter]. The mood darkens. Now every coin matters. The cat doubles down.
*(Goal: a money target with a sense of urgency. Stakes go up.)*

### ACT 4 — "The Hard Winter" (the setback)
The emotional low point. Pick one or more:
- Winter comes — fewer mice, harder work, the house is freezing.
- The cat gets hurt on a job and has to recover (Dr. Amara).
- A job goes wrong / a setback costs coins.
The family almost gives up hope — but they rally *around the cat* and each other.
*(Goal: push through adversity; the community starts helping back.)*

### ACT 5 — "Home"
The final push. The neighbours, now friends, pitch in. The deadline is met. The house is warm, full, alive. The last scene: the family — and the cat — finally safe, together, by a warm fire. Mr. Crick maybe even softens.
*(Goal: reach the final milestone. Emotional payoff.)*

> **Optional epilogue / endless mode:** after the house is saved, keep playing to make it a *dream* home, help other struggling neighbours, or adopt another stray.

## 8. The House Progression System 🏚️→🏡

> This is the heart of "realism" and the player's sense of progress. As coins/milestones are reached, the house **visibly upgrades** on screen. Players should be able to *see* their work paying off.

| Tier | Coins (suggested) | What the player SEES | What it MEANS |
|------|------|----------------------|---------------|
| 0 — Struggling | 0 | Broken fence, leaky roof, bare dim room, empty table, one flickering bulb | "Hungry & cold…" |
| 1 — Patched up | ~15 | Fence fixed, roof patched, door no longer broken | "A little hopeful…" |
| 2 — Warmth | ~40 | Warm lights in the windows, smoke from the chimney, food on the table | "Getting warmer 🙂" |
| 3 — Comfort | ~80 | New furniture, curtains, a rug, the kids have toys | "Getting better" |
| 4 — Growing | ~140 | A little garden grows, flowers in window boxes, fresh paint | "Happy & hopeful 😊" |
| 5 — Home | ~220 | Bright, full, beautiful home; family gathered, laughing, fire lit | "Safe. Together. Home. ❤️" |

> 💡 Tie the **family-status text** and **family member moods** to these tiers too: hungry → smiling → laughing. Maybe each tier unlocks a little cutscene of the family reacting.

## 10. Key Emotional Beats (the moments players will remember)

1. **The adoption in the rain** — Lily, soaked, holding the shivering cat: *"Mum, please. He has no one. Like us, kind of."*
2. **The overheard whisper** — the cat, awake at night, hears the parents quietly worry about rent. The decision is born here.
3. **The first coin home** — the cat drops a coin at the family's feet. They don't understand how — but they cry, and laugh, and call it a little miracle.
4. **The deadline** — Mr. Crick's visit. The room goes quiet. Lily hugs the cat tighter.
5. **The setback** — the cat hurt / the cold night. The family takes care of *the cat* for once — showing the love goes both ways.
6. **The town pitches in** — the neighbours show up to help, because the cat helped them first.
7. **The final hearth scene** — warm house, full bellies, everyone (and the cat) safe and together.

**THE ENDING — by the fire**
- LILY *(hugging the cat)*: "You did this. Somehow… you did this for us."
- CAT *(thought)*: *"No, Lily. We did it. This is what a family does."*
